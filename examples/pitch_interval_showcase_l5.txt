Comparing those distances gives the quality double-diminished.
So the interval is a double-diminished eleventh.

ANSWER:

COT:
Start with a double-diminished twelfth: interval number 12 and 17 semitones.
For interval numbers, addition uses current + interval - 1, and subtraction uses current - interval + 1.
For reduce-then-invert, first reduce compound interval numbers by subtracting 7 for each octave; then invert the simple interval so the two simple interval numbers add to 9.
Reduce a double-diminished twelfth to a double-diminished fifth; then invert it: 9 - 5 = 4, and double-diminished inverts to double-augmented.
Add a perfect twelfth: interval number 4 + 12 - 1 = 15, and semitones 7 + 19 = 26.
Subtract an augmented sixth: interval number 15 - 6 + 1 = 10, and semitones 26 - 10 = 16.
Subtract a double-augmented seventh: interval number 10 - 7 + 1 = 4, and semitones 16 - 13 = 3.
With interval number 4 and 3 semitones, the resulting interval is a double-diminished fourth.
